Cod-GADHUS MORHUA

The Atlantic cod, Gadus morhua, is a commercially important fish species found in the North Atlantic Ocean. It is known for its firm meat and is a popular food source, often enjoyed fresh, dried, salted, or smoked. Cod can be found in various marine habitats, from shallow coastal waters to deeper continental shelves, and undergoes seasonal migrations

SAITHE(POLLACHIUS VIRENS)

It is generally referred to in the United States as pollock. commonly known in Britain as the coalfish, coley, or saithe.This species can be separated from P. pollachius by looking at the relative lengths of the upper and lower jaws. P. pollachius has a longer underslung lower jaw while P. virens has approximately equal upper and lower jaw lengths. This gives a very different profile to the head. In general, P. pollachius is a brown or golden colour with a dark back while P. virens is bright silver with a very dark green back. P. virens generally appears to have relatively smaller eyes,they are found in scotland or England.
